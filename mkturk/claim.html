Instructions

The task here is to annotate tweets in relation to a topic where you have to annotate what kind of claim a tweet contains if any.
A claim is standpoint towards the topic being discussed either directly or indirectly. The claim should be able to clearly be identified in a tweet on its own without relying on an assumption from the reader. For response tweets this is an important issue as the user might implicitly be supporting a claim relevant for the topic, or adding a claim to a stance on the topic, such tweets should be annotated as not containing a claim. The claim should also clearly have either a postive stance or negative stance towards the discussed topic. If it does not have a clear stance towards the topic, it should be marked as unreleated.

Select the class you best thinks describes the tweet:
Classes Guidance
Implicit An implicit claim is where the claim is not syntastically linked to the topic. This means that the tweet is claiming something for a similar topic or the same topic but is not explictly mentioning parts of the topic.
An example of this:
Topic: We should add a carbon tax to food production?
Tweet: "@mention CAN WE GET RID OF THE STUPID CARBON TAX"
The tweet leaves out any mention of food production of any sort, so could be making a claim about a similar topic like carbon taxes on fossile fuels, and therefore should be marked as implicit.
If you are not sure if the claim is implicit or explicit, mark it as implicit.
Explicit An explicit claim is clearly linked fully with the topic so every main point in the topic is covered by the tweets claim.
An example of this:
Topic: Meat alternatives should be subsidized
Tweet: "@mention It would also be nice if our government could begin subsidizing more sustainable options (like plant based meat) vs things like beef but... i digress"
Here the tweet is clearly talking about all parts of the topic while having a clear stance towards the topic.
Unrelated The tweet contains a claim that is clearly discussing a different topic, or it contains a claim that is not clear in its stance towards the topic.
An example of this:
Topic: We should subsidize green nutrition
Tweet: "@mention @mention Green taxes go into subsidizing development and production of green energy solutions. If we were on 100% renewables, our electricity prices would not have needed to go up. We need to move into self-sufficient green energy as soon as possible"
The tweet has clearly claims about green energy but the topic is about green nutrition and it is therefore unrelated to the topic.
No Claim The tweet does not contain a claim at all. Any tweets where none of the above classes fit should be marked as no claim
